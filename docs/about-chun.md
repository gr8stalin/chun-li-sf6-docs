The strike second queen. Simple to start with due to her great walkspeed and pokes, Chun uses movement and pokes to escort opponents to the corner. Less of an all-rounder versus a character like Ryu due to fewer gorilla tactics and less safety on her normals and specials, but fills the same niche in a more defensive and strategic manner, having an answer to almost every aggressive option in the game.

In Street Fighter 6, her new addition is "Serenity Stream", a stance that gives her access to extra normals that allow her to extend pressure or combo from otherwise uncancelable moves.

## What is she good at?

As a limb zoner, Chun is good at screen control. She's not as good as Dhalsim, Guile, or JP at this, but her movement speed is what makes her limb zoning safer and more threatening. She can use her faster backwalk to wiggle just out of range of normals or use her faster forward walkspeed to interrupt her opponents with lights and sometimes mediums.

Chun is optimized to be a defensive but forward-moving wall that uses presence and the threat of counterplay to push her opponent to the dreaded corner. She is possibly the only character in the game with an armory of moves that can either counter or at least alleviate every other character's primary gimmick and defends against the system well: 

- instant air legs counters throw loops meterlessly with great reward
- her stand lights tend to be a blanket answer to checking drive rush with easy anti-air access
- stance helps her duck fireballs and set up stance medium punch (slide from SF5) as a counter fireball
- has every anti-air option: grounded and jump normals, air throw, air-invuln anti-air special

Her corner pressure takes some getting used to but is the gold standard of strike/throw/shimmy in fighting games. When you corner the opponent as Chun, you are upholding fighting game tradition as your offense.

## Where is she weak?

Sorely lacking in forceful aggression and a good drive rush, she struggles against other characters that are easy to play defensively like Ed or Lily, or are defensive by nature as she is like Dhalsim and Guile.

*Very* meter hungry and mechanically dependent for her players to do the absolute basics of oki. Since you'll often be standing while playing Chun in order to take advantage of her mobility, you must master counterhit confirming stand light punch into crouch medium kick so you can do spinning bird kick for meterless oki while standing. Otherwise, you'll have to spend meter on od legs or drive rush cancels. A recent buff gave her counterhit confirms into stance medium punch from several mediums and stand heavy punch.

The cons of having an answer to everything in neutral means you need to manage everything to a higher standard right off the bat.
