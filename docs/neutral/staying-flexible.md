In SF6, Chun does not have forceful offense outside 2MK drive rush cancel. In contrast, she has good "in the pocket" defense against close range drive rush, dash, poke pressure, and jumps.

One thing I've found as a way to stay stable but flexible in tense neutral situations is to keep it light: specifically, base your gameplan around the LK button. 22LK is your anti-air with oki, 5LK checks dashes and drive rushes without as much fear of awkward hitbox interactions, and both are 5 frame startup. What's nice about 5LK is that your fingers are already in the area to use 5LP for checks/pressure alongside 5LK, and this primes you for both grounded and air throws as well.

You're not going to anti-air every jumpin, and anti-airs versus divekicks are going to be uncommon, but you want to minimize both what you're looking for and what your answer is to it. 5LK as a grounded check isn't exceptionally powerful on its own, but I've found it checks a lot of drive rush buttons without getting into a counter-hit trade situation (almost all counter-hit trades are unfavorable for Chun). 

You can apply a similar strategy to her mediums at further distances, where you focus on 4MP for drive rush checks and 22MK for antiairs. I'm not as much of a fan of this because you have to think a lot more about hitbox interactions from drive rush and 22MK's oki isn't as good as 22LK's or 22HK's, but this one also allows you to focus on offense via 4MP and 2MK as pokes.

I've seen other people use 4HP as a midscreen drive rush offense check but the relatively slow startup and short reach of 4HP combined with how many things can be thrown at you in SF6 makes it a poor choice in my opinion. Just like everything else in fighting games, it can work out when you have an idea of what's coming your way and have a read on your opponent, but it's not portable; better players will catch on to your positioning and button choice, unaware/"ranked" players will change their approach and button choices on a whim.
