# Fireball Drive Rush counterplay

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/1qxv9GrdklE?si=ZknwRgtzpAw-zQJR" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

The layer 1 strategy of fireball/OD fireball -> drive rush is solved with a combination of OD hazanshu (214KK) and stance slide (214P~MP).

For AKI's OD fireball, you want to time OD hazanshu around the instant you see the fireball/poison bubble start to form around AKI's hands, not exactly when she glows yellow. If you OD hazanshu too early, there's a chance you might get hit by the fireball. 

If OD hazanshu hits, the following punishes are available:

- 22HK (HK tensho kick)
- [2]8LK (LK sbk)
- 2HK (sweep)
- Super 1, 2, or 3/CA

If you keep delaying after the fireball is on screen and they drive rush, ex hazanshu will hit counterhit and you can either follow up with a funny 2MP that air resets them or cancel into any super.

Stance slide is the less risky but overall less rewarding way to stop AKI from going green behind a fireball. You'll need to be a distance away from the AKI player because going into stance has its own startup frames, but once you're in stance, you can react to whatever they choose to do to you: poke with stance MK or stance LK, check the fireball drive rush with stance MK, and antiair with stance HK.

Once the AKI adapts (if they do), going into stance will be more difficult because they'll be aware it's happening and attempt to mess with your reactions by spacing jumps and taking advantage of the fact that stance robs you of Chun's greatest tool, movement.
